The 4 Enharmonic Pairs

Carnatic music has 16 swarasthana names but only 12 unique pitch positions. Four pairs of swarasthana names share the same frequency. In Melakarta system, when building a raga you pick from the R-name column OR the G/D/N-name column — never both, because they are the same note.

Why Just Intonation?

Equal Temperament vs Just Intonation

A piano divides the octave into 12 perfectly equal semitones. Every interval is slightly "out of tune" by a small amount, but the same for every key — convenient for modulation.

Carnatic music uses Just Intonation: intervals are pure harmonic ratios derived from the overtone series (1/1, 9/8, 5/4, 4/3, 3/2…). These create beatless, resonant consonances. The intervals are not equally spaced — the semitone between Sa and Suddha Ri (16/15) is different in size from the semitone between Suddha Ri and Chatusruti Ri (135/128).

In MIDI export, RagaNotate compensates for equal-temperament playback using pitch bend events to hit the exact JI frequencies.
